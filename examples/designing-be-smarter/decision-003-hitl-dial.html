Decision 3 of 6 · The Dial

How does the human get into (and out of) the loop?

Decision-kit is interactive by default — that's its identity and, per your framing, its adoption tax. /be-smarter inverts the premise: "most folks just want the model to be smarter." So headless is the default, and this decision designs the argument that lets a human step in.

Builds on Decisions 1–2: the double diamond has two natural pause points (converge on framing, converge on picks), and the triage score from Decision 2 gives any adaptive variant a stakes signal for free.
Research Context
  • Production HITL practice converges on three modes: approval gates at defined points, escalation on uncertainty/risk, and post-action audit trails. Most real systems need all three — a single on/off toggle is below the floor of current practice. understandingdata.com HITL patterns, digitalapplied 2026 escalation guide
  • Escalation thresholds should depend on risk tier and context, not a global setting — stricter in high-stakes domains, looser in low-stakes ones, tuned over time. The triage score from Decision 2 is exactly this signal, already computed. Anna Jey, production HITL patterns (Medium)
  • Pauses must not lose state. The interrupt pattern is checkpoint → wait → resume from checkpoint, never re-run from scratch. For a thinking skill: whatever was diverged/converged before the pause must survive the human's response verbatim. Redis HITL production patterns
  • Decision-kit already shipped both proven interaction shapes. /autodecide's batch-review (run everything, pause once at the end, override by exception) and /state-your-case's circuit breaker (interrupt only when a judgment call is too important to decide silently). The dial doesn't need inventing — it needs assembling.
  • Reviewer fatigue is the documented failure of always-on approval. Humans rubber-stamp when asked too often ("automation complacency") — pause points must be few and high-value, or the loop becomes theater. strata.io HITL oversight guide
Options
A

Binary toggle

--human on or off. Off (default): fully headless. On: pause at both converge points with full decision pages, decision-kit style.

offheadless, end-to-end
--humanpause at framing + at picks, pages rendered

Simplest to spec, document, and understand

Matches the user's literal ask ("a human in the loop argument")

No middle ground — it's rubber-stamp-everything or trust-everything

Below the three-mode floor of current HITL practice

★ Recommended
B

Three-position dial

--human=off|review|full. Off (default): headless. Review: run the whole protocol autonomously, pause once at the end with a batch-review of every call made — override by exception (the /autodecide pattern). Full: pause at the two converge points.

offheadless; trace logged (Decision 4's artifact)
reviewone pause at the end; "looks good" or "for X I want Y"
fullpause at framing converge + picks converge

Review is the sweet spot: human judgment without blocking the thinking

Both interactive positions reuse decision-kit's proven UX patterns verbatim

Few, high-value pauses — respects the reviewer-fatigue finding

Three positions to document and test instead of two

Fixed positions — can't say "only bother me if it matters"

C

Adaptive interrupts

No positions — the model pulls the human in only when it matters: triage score above threshold, a tripwire fires, or options converge too close to call. --interrupt-at=8 tunes the threshold. /state-your-case as the default interaction model.

silentmost tasks: no interruption at all
⚡ interruptstakes ≥ threshold or tripwire → checkpoint, ask, resume

"Only bother me when it matters" — the ideal for busy users

Triage score makes the threshold context-aware for free

Unpredictable: users can't know in advance whether they'll be interrupted

Trust must be earned — a misfiring threshold (either direction) sours v1 adoption

D

Interactive default, --auto to opt out

Decision-kit parity: the human is in the loop unless they say otherwise. Preserves the kit's judgment-first philosophy in the new skill.

defaultpause at converge points with pages
--autoheadless

Philosophically continuous with decision-kit's decision gate

Contradicts the founding premise — "most folks just want the model to be smarter"

Kills always-on usage; re-imports the adoption tax this project exists to remove

Recommendation: B. The review position is the genuinely new value — full human judgment, zero blocking — and both interactive positions are battle-tested decision-kit UX. C is the right v2: once the triage rubric has earned trust, --human=auto becomes a fourth dial position using the same threshold machinery. Designing B's spec so C can slot in later costs nothing now.
